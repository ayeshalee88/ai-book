# Course Syllabus: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Course Duration: 13 Weeks

This comprehensive course covers the principles of Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics, integrating theoretical concepts with practical implementation. The curriculum is designed to take students from foundational concepts to advanced applications in embodied intelligence.

:::tip Course Overview
This 13-week journey will transform you from a curious learner to a skilled practitioner in Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics. Each week builds upon the previous, creating a solid foundation for advanced applications.
:::

## Weekly Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Learning Objectives | Key Activities |
|------|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| 1 | Introduction to Physical AI & Embodied Intelligence | Understand core concepts of embodied AI and why physical interaction matters for intelligence | Readings, conceptual exercises |
| 2-3 | Sensorimotor Systems & Perception | Learn sensorimotor coupling principles and computer vision for physical interaction | Simulation exercises, perception pipeline implementation |
| 4-5 | Reinforcement Learning for Robot Control | Master RL algorithms for locomotion and manipulation | Gazebo simulations, RL training projects |
| 6-7 | Sim-to-Real Transfer Techniques | Explore methods for bridging simulation and real-world deployment | Transfer learning projects, domain randomization |
| 8-10 | Humanoid Robot Development | Study kinematics, dynamics, and control systems for bipedal locomotion | Kinematic modeling, balance control implementation |
| 11-12 | Advanced Manipulation & Interaction | Focus on grasping, dexterity, and human-robot interaction | Manipulation tasks, HRI design projects |
| 13 | Conversational Robotics | Integrate GPT models and multi-modal interaction | Capstone project implementation |

## Assessment Structure

| Component | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Weekly Assignments | 30% | Practical exercises and simulations |
| Midterm Project | 25% | ROS 2 package development |
| Final Project | 35% | Capstone: Simulated humanoid robot with conversational AI |
| Participation | 10% | Engagement in discussions and peer reviews |

## Prerequisites

- Basic understanding of robotics and AI concepts
- Programming experience (preferably Python)
- Familiarity with mathematical concepts (linear algebra, calculus)
- Some exposure to control theory (helpful but not required)

:::note Getting Started
New to Physical AI? Start with Week 1 materials and work systematically through each module. The course is designed to build knowledge progressively, with each week's content depending on previous concepts.
:::
