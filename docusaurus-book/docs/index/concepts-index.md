## Alphabetical Term Definitions

### Actuator
Mechanical devices that convert energy into physical motion. In robotics, actuators control the movement of joints and other mechanical components.

### Affordance
An opportunity for interaction that is provided by an environment to an agent. In robotics, affordances represent the action possibilities available to a robot based on its capabilities and the environment's features.

### Embodied Cognition
A theory suggesting that cognitive processes are deeply rooted in the body's interactions with the world. This perspective emphasizes that the body's form and sensorimotor experiences contribute to cognitive processes.

### Forward Kinematics
The process of calculating the position and orientation of a robot's end-effector (typically the hand or tool) based on the known joint angles.

### Inverse Kinematics
The process of determining the joint angles required to achieve a desired position and orientation of a robot's end-effector.

### Locomotion
The ability to move from one place to another. In robotics, locomotion refers to the various methods and control strategies that enable robots to move through their environment.

### Morphological Computation
The idea that part of the computation required for intelligent behavior is performed by the physical form of the system, rather than entirely by the controller.

### Reinforcement Learning
A type of machine learning where an agent learns to make decisions by performing actions and receiving rewards or penalties, with the goal of maximizing cumulative reward.

### Sensorimotor Coupling
The tight integration between sensory input and motor output, where perception and action are closely linked and influence each other in real-time.

### Sim-to-Real Transfer
The process of transferring policies or behaviors learned in simulation to real-world robotic systems, often requiring techniques to address the reality gap.
